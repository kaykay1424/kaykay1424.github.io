My Profile

Hobbies: Basketball, Soccer, Music (hip hop, r&b, pop, rock, soul, salsa, bachata, merengue, sertanejo, pretty much everything), Movies (action,comedy,romance), Learning languages (both computer and human)

Ever since I had the opportunity to live in South Korea for 2 years and discover a new language, I have loved learning languages as well as traveling and learning about new cultures. Over the years I have dabbled in 8 languages, such as French and Korean, and after 9 years of studying Spanish and 5 years of studying Chinese, I have reached an advanced level of proficiency in both languages.

My language studies began when I was in high school in South Korea but peaked while I attended the University of Tennessee at Knoxille where I majored in International Business and Spanish with a Chinese minor. During my college career, I had many language learning experiences. I was involved in language and culture groups, such as the Latin American Student Organization and the Chinese Culture Club. From my sophomore to my junior year, I worked at the International House on campus that provided cultural programs, such as the World Showcase, the International Festival, and Language Tables. I participated in several of these programs, especially the Spanish and Chinese Language Tables. During the summer of my sophomore year, I studied abroad in Costa Rica for a month where I improved my fluency in Spanish and gained a good cultural understanding of the Costa Rican people by living with a host family and adhering to a Spanish-only policy. During my junior year, I helped interpret for UT’s Immigration Clinic during a few legal interviews and taught a few courses at Knoxville’s Hispanic Center to Spanish-speaking students. During my senior year, I helped interpret for the Center for Sport, Peace, and Society during a tour of UT’s academic facilities given to a group of Venezuelan coaches. Also during this year, I began working as a volunteer translator for the website Watching America where I would translate 1 article giving an opinion on the U.S. or an issue related to the U.S. from Spanish to English every 2 weeks.

Additionally, during this time, I worked as a tutor of Spanish and Chinese where I was not only able to solidify my knowledge of the two languages but was also able to help my tutees considerably improve their performance in their courses. After graduating, I spent 2 months in China on the Critical Language Scholarship program where I gained a great understanding of Chinese society, culture, and history and significantly improved my Chinese skills by adhering to a Chinese-only policy and living with a host family, like I did in Costa Rica.

Upon returning to the U.S., I continued translating for Watching America and obtained a bilingual customer service job at Farmers Insurance that allowed me to significantly improve my Spanish speaking skills by interpreting from Spanish to English and English to Spanish for customers and Farmers staff. I now hope to gain more experience and pursue a career in the translation field.
